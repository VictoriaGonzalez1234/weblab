An All-Girls Education

The Hockaday School is a prestigious college- preparatory day and boarding school located in Dallas, Texas. Founded in 1913 by Ela Hockaday, it is one of the oldest and most respected girls' schools in the United States. The school serves girls from pre-kindergarten through 12th grade, with a focus on academic excellence and character building.

The Cornerstones: Courtesy & Character

The Hockaday School places a strong emphasis on the development of courtesy and character as key components of its educational philosophy. This focus is rooted in the belief that academic excellence should be paired with moral integrity, kindness, and social responsibility. The school integrates these values into its curriculum and daily life through several approaches.

Core Values: Scholarship & Athletics

The Hockaday School emphasizes a balanced approach to education by fostering both Scholarship and Athletics as core values. The school encourages and promotes athletic achievement, recognizing the importance of physical fitness, teamwork, and perseverance. By integrating both scholarship and athletics, Hockaday aims to develop well-rounded individuals who excel not only in the classroom but also in their personal and physical development.
